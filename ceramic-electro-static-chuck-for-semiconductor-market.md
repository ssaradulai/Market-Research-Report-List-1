<p><p>Ceramic Electrostatic Chucks (ESCs) are critical in semiconductor manufacturing, enhancing wafer stability during processes. The Coulomb type employs electrostatic forces to hold the wafer, providing uniform pressure and reduced defects. In contrast, the Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) type utilizes a combination of electrostatic and van der Waals forces, offering improved adhesion and flexibility at various pressures. Both types contribute to advanced fabrication techniques, catering to diverse semiconductor needs and driving market demand for efficient and reliable wafer handling solutions.</p></p>
