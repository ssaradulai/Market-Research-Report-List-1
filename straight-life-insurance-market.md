<p><ul><li>Non-participating Whole Life</li><li>Participating Whole Life</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Straight life insurance refers to long-term policies providing lifetime coverage with fixed premiums. Non-participating whole life policies have guaranteed benefits but do not pay dividends to policyholders. Participating whole life policies, on the other hand, allow policyholders to receive dividends based on the insurer's performance, offering potential cash value growth. Other market types include term life insurance, which offers coverage for a specified period without cash value, and universal life insurance, providing flexible premiums and death benefits.</p></p>
