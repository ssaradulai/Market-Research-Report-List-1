<p><p>A pulse oximeter is a non-invasive medical device that measures the oxygen saturation level in a person's blood, along with heart rate. It typically attaches to a fingertip or earlobe and uses light absorption characteristics of oxygenated and deoxygenated hemoglobin to provide readings. The device is widely used in hospitals, clinics, and even at home, making it essential for monitoring respiratory conditions, chronic diseases, and during surgical procedures.</p><p>The Pulse Oximeter Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.2% during the forecast period, driven by increasing awareness of respiratory diseases, the rise in geriatric populations, and the need for continuous health monitoring. Additionally, the COVID-19 pandemic has heightened interest in pulse oximeters as a critical tool for assessing respiratory health, leading to a surge in demand.</p><p>Recent trends in the market include the development of advanced, portable, and consumer-friendly devices that can connect to smartphones for real-time monitoring and data sharing.
