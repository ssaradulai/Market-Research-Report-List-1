<p><ul><li>Picosecond Solid State Laser</li><li>Nanosecond Solid State Laser</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Solid-state lasers for laser processing equipment are categorized into two main types: picosecond and nanosecond solid-state lasers. Picosecond lasers emit ultrafast pulses, typically lasting one trillionth of a second, allowing for precise material removal with minimal heat-affected zones, making them ideal for intricate applications like micro-machining. In contrast, nanosecond lasers operate with pulse durations in the range of one billionth of a second, suitable for tasks requiring higher energy output for cutting and engraving on various materials.</p></p>
