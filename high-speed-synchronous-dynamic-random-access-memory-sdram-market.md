<p><p>High Speed Synchronous Dynamic Random Access Memory (SDRAM) is designed for rapid data retrieval and processing, crucial for modern computing needs. The market is categorized into Double Data Rate (DDR) and Single Data Rate (SDR) types. DDR SDRAM transfers data on both the rising and falling edges of the clock cycle, enabling higher bandwidth and improved performance. In contrast, SDR SDRAM transmits data on only one edge, offering slower speeds. The DDR segment has largely dominated due to its efficiency and faster transfer rates.</p></p>
